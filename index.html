1. Tony's "Panic Attack" Gabagool Special

Ingredients

  • Gabagool (Capicola): 1/4 lb, thinly sliced (skip the nitrate worries)
  • Cheese: Sharp Provolone
  • Bread: Semolina roll or a crusty Italian loaf from Russo's Bakery (call moltisanti for support)
  • Vinegar Peppers: A handful of hot cherry peppers or roasted red peppers
  • Dressing: Extra Virgin Olive Oil and a splash of Red Wine Vinegar
  • Seasoning: Dried oregano and a pinch of salt (to not let your blood pressure drop)

Steps

  1. Prep the Bread: Slice that roll open. If the bread is fresh, leave it alone. If it's day-old, toast it lightly—but don't burn it, or Paulie will get upset (he can't take any more betrayal)
  2. The Foundation: Drizzle the olive oil and vinegar directly onto the bread so it soaks in. Sprinkle your oregano here
  3. Layering: Fold the Gabagool slices. Don't just lay them flat
  4. Cheese: Layer the sharp provolone on top of the meat
  5. Heat: Top with your vinegar peppers
  6. Finish: Close the sandwich. Press it down with the palm of your hand . Enjoy it

Behind The Scenes

Trivia : The word was also used for comedic effect in an episode of The Office, where Michael Scott mispronounces various Italian meats ("Cap-uh-coal," "Mort-ah-dell," "Coca-coal")

"Gabagool? Over here!"

2. The Big Kahuna Burger

Ingredients

  • Beef: 1 lb Ground Chuck
  • Glaze: Teriyaki sauce (store-bought or homemade)
  • Pineapple: Grilled pineapple rings (this makes it Kahuna)
  • Cheese: Monterey Jack
  • Bun: King's Hawaiian Rolls or a soft brioche bun, toasted
  • Garnish: Caramelized onions, lettuce, and a little mayo
  • Beverage: A Sprite to wash it all down

Steps

  1. Heat: Get your cast-iron skillet screaming hot
  2. Patties: Form the beef into balls, then smash them flat into the pan. Salt and pepper heavily
  3. Aloha: While the burgers sizzle, throw those pineapple rings on the grill or in the side of the pan until they get those nice char marks
  4. Glaze: Just before the burgers are done, brush them generously with the teriyaki sauce. Let it caramelize on the meat
  5. Meltdown: Place the Monterey Jack on the patties. Cover the pan for 30 seconds to steam the cheese
  6. Assembly: Toast the buns. Bottom bun, mayo, lettuce, burger patty, grilled pineapple, caramelized onions, top bun
  7. Test: Take a bite

Behind The Scenes

Trivia: The Tarantino Universe The "Big Kahuna Burger" is a fictional chain that doesn't just appear in Pulp Fiction. It shows up in Reservoir Dogs, From Dusk Till Dawn, and even Four Rooms.

3. Dorsia's Sea Urchin Ceviche

Ingredients

  • Protein: 100g Grade-A Hokkaido Uni (Sea Urchin)
  • Acid: Fresh Yuzu juice, imported. Not lemon. Never lemon
  • Oil: White Truffle Oil (just a droplet)
  • Crunch: One single radish, sliced so thin it is transparent
  • Garnish: Edible 24-karat gold leaf and micro-cilantro
  • Seasoning: Maldon sea salt flakes
  • Pairing: A glass of Chardonnay. Oaked. Expensive

Steps

  1. Preparation: Clean the sea urchin tongues with the precision of a surgeon. Do not bruise them
  2. Cure: In a crystal mixing bowl, gently toss the uni in the yuzu juice. Let it sit for exactly 45 seconds
  3. Plating: Use silver tweezers to place the uni onto a chilled ceramic plate. Minimalism is key. Negative space is your friend
  4. Anointing: Apply the truffle oil using an eyedropper
  5. Aesthetics: Place the radish slice and the gold leaf (It needs to look better than Paul Allen's appetizer)
  6. Consumption: Don't just stare at it, eat it

Behind The Scenes

Trivia: the restaurant "Dorsia" represents the ultimate unreachable goal. Patrick Bateman can get the suits, the apartment, and the body, but he can never get a reservation at Dorsia. It is the one status symbol that eludes him, driving his insecurity wild.

"I have to return some videotapes."
— Patrick Bateman
